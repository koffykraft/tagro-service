Traditional Preparations

Documented household and community preparations from Ethiopia and Indonesia — presented as observation, per the Foundation page's Evidence Discipline, not as instruction or proof of effect.

Engere

A milk-based coffee leaf beverage from Gofa Zone, South Ethiopia. Documented across 385 households. Three preparation types, ten spice ingredients, and a safety record that surprised researchers.

Ethiopia 4 pages

Chemo

A roasted coffee leaf brew from Tepi Town, Southwestern Ethiopia. Always spiced, always served with food, always social. Fire is the defining step. Documented across 64 households.

Ethiopia 5 pages

Kuti

A coffee leaf brew from the walled city of Harar made from fallen yellowed leaves. Almost no caffeine. Part of a whole-plant coffee culture embedded in Harari household life and women's land ownership.

Ethiopia 4 pages

Kawa Daun

A smoked coffee leaf brew from the Minangkabau highlands of West Sumatra. Leaves smoked over cinnamon wood, brewed long and dark. A tradition that survived Dutch colonial prohibition.

Buna Culinary

Door Seven

Coffee leaf as a culinary ingredient — what it does when salt, fat, acid, and heat are applied, alone and in combination. New territory. No established reference exists. These pages are offered as foundational theory and working preparations for testing and collaborative development.

The Leaf at the Table

The governing frame for Buna as a culinary ingredient. Three modes — extraction, whole leaf, transformative additive — and six elements. For anyone who thinks in a kitchen before they cook in it.

Door Seven Page 1

The Leaf and the Elements — Theory

What happens to the whole leaf under salt, fat, acid, and heat — alone and in combination. Sensory outcomes mapped across taste, aroma, texture, mouthfeel, and aftertaste. Clearly marked as theory with no published culinary reference. Offered for testing.

Door Seven Page 2 Theory

The Preparations

Seventeen foundational preparations from simple infusion through the Kerala table pathway, roasted leaf, and comparative tasting sets. Quantities, ratios, and tasting notes. Designed to be repeatable and built upon.

Door Seven Page 3

Concepts — Eight Methods, One Leaf

How food cultures absorb new ingredients — and what that logic offers for Buna. Eight universal cooking concepts, each with a traditional reference, a note on how it has been absorbed across cultures, and the question it opens for the cook with Buna on their counter.

Door Seven Page 4 Concepts

Buna in Kerala — A Concept Canvas

Eight positions at the Kerala table — rice, kanji, kappa, the rasam position, the moru position, kanji vellum, the sadya, the morning cup. Each described on its own terms. Then one question: if Buna were here, what would it need to be?
